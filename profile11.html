Meme Decay/Degenerator

Generation loss is the gradual decay of image quality and clarity over time, ultimately resulting in the loss of the copied information. This process is caused by a combination of factors related to the original copy, the copying process, and environmental conditions. Meme decay a live exploration of digital decay, a process perhaps most familiar today through the aesthetic of "deep-fried memes." It takes a single image and subjects it to a relentless loop of lossy compression. In each frame, the image is saved as a low-quality JPEG, then immediately reloaded, simulating the generational loss that occurs as files are repeatedly shared and re-encoded across digital networks. I view it as a memento mori pointing to lifespan of internet content and a commentary on the absurdity of digital permanence.
